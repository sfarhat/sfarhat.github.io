Facebook
pronouned: face-book
Definition:
1. A website where you can connect with friends, share photos, and hear some news.
Examples:
1. "Hey, do you have a Facebook account? We should become friends on there so we can talk!"

Twitter
pronouned: twit-ur
Definition:
1. A website where you can type a paragraph up to 250 letters, which people can read them, say they like it, and 're-tweet' it, meaning sharing it with others.
Examples:
1. "Have you seen what President Trump tweeted?"

Snapchat
pronouned: snap-chat
Definition:
1. An phone app where you can send friends photos or videos (also called snaps) which disappear after 1-10 seconds.
2. If you see someone making weird faces at their phones for a moment then typing quickly, they are probably on Snapchat.
Examples:
1. "No way! You're going to Italy? Make sure to send me snaps of everything!"

Instagram
pronouned: in-stuh-gram
Definition:
1. A phone app where users post higher quality photos of various subjects that others can see, like, and comment on."
2. When someone says they are "following" someone on Instagram, that means they are notified each time that person posts something.
3. A common alternative is simply the word "insta". "Finsta" is short for 'fake Instagram', and is usually a private account where someone may post embarassing photos just for close friends to see.

Tinder
pronouned: tin-dur
Definition:
1. A popular phone app for dating, where users see pictures and descriptions of people in a couple mile radius who are also using the app.
2. Users have the option to "swipe right or left" on someone's photo, signifying if they are interested or not in that person. If both parties swipe right, they are connected and can directly text each other via the app.
Examples:
1. "Yeah I'm going on a Tinder date tonight. We both swiped right and we really hit it off as we were texting!"

Tumblr
pronouned: tum-blur
Definition:
1. A popular website that is mainly used for blogging.
2. Users can post their own photos or thoughts, or 're-blog' others' that they enjoy. Any blogged or re-blogged items show up on the user's profile page.
Examples:
1. "I got distracted for 3 hours scrolling through Tumblr. People post such interesting things!"

Bae
pronouned: bay
Definition:
1. Stands for Before Anyone Else, used to describe someone very close, such as a good friend or significant other. A less common alternative is "boo".
Examples:
1. "Jane is the kindest friend I have ever had; she is literally bae."
2. "Sorry guys, I can't hang out tonight; I going out with bae."

Meme
pronouned: meem
Definition:
1. An internet trend that can come from a funny photo, TV show, movie, funny event, etc. and is usually a photo or video.
Examples:
1. After Pepsi's recent commercial, people inserted Pepsi cans into photos from the American Civil Rights movement to poke fun at the commercial's display of Pepsi being a unifying force to combat police brutality.

Doggo
pronouned: dog-oh
Definition:
1. It is an endearing term used to describe dogs that many consider cute.
2. It extends to the word "pupper" for puppy as well. Sometimes, "woofer" is used in place of "doggo", and "bork" instead of bark.
Examples:
1. "Oh my gosh, look at that doggo over there. I want to pet it so bad!"
